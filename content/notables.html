Rosalia de Castro

Rosalía de Castro was a Galician writer who promoted and valued literature in this language. Considered a fundamental author of the 19th century, she died young, a victim of uterine cancer. Throughout various trips and changes of address, she wrote several works of great quality, both poetic and narrative. The best known of hers, hers 'Follas Novas'.

Rosalía de Castro was born in the city of Santiago de Compostela on February 24, 1837. The daughter of anoblewoman and a priest, she was considered a poor birth, so at first she was not accepted by her mother's family. Therefore, during her early years, she was raised by her two paternal aunts.

At the age of five, she moved with her mother to the town of Padrón (La Coruña). Her adolescence was marked by a deep life crisis after the discovery of her illegitimate daughter status and by a delicate health that she never improved.



Suso de Toro

Spanish writer, Suso de Toro, named Xesús Miguel de Toro Santos, graduated in Geography and History, specializing in Modern and Contemporary Art at the University of Santiago de Compostela. Throughout his professional career he has worked as a secondary school teacher. De Toro directed the Pepa Loba theater group, and combines his literary work with the creation of scripts for radio and television, as well as collaborating in newspapers such as La Voz de Galicia, El País or La Vanguardia.

De Toro writes in Galician and his novel Thirteen chimes has been made into a film. Among his literary production are other titles such as Sleepwalkers, Seven Words, Out of Himself or An Elegant Gentleman. In 2010, he announced his retirement from literature to devote himself to his teaching work. He has won several awards, including the National Narrative Award in 2003.
